Realization of Optical Dipole Traps and Tweezer Array

With the advent of advanced cooling techniques, new areas of studies using neutral atoms opened up. An optical dipole trap serves as a reservoir for single atoms, which can be used to precisely load a tweezer. These atoms can further be used as sensors, by exciting them to their Rydberg states. An array of such atoms can be used to simulate quantum systems as well as many-body physics. These arrays have also been proposed to be used as a quantum processor with each of the atoms acting like a qubit. Gate operations using these arrays have been reported and advancements are being made. Getting exposed to the techniques and participating in experiemnt of such calibre was the aim of this project.

Sensing prospects of Tapered region in Optical Micro/nano fibers using visible spectrum

Sensing is a crucial technological application especially in fields of atmospheric monitoring, health diagnostics and other relevant fileds like magnetometry and real-time monitoring of civil structures. Optical fibers have been a crucial for these applications. Typical proposed OMNFs and integrated OMNF structures use only waist uniform region. In this project, we aimed at exploring the prospects of tapered region, which hosts local diameter variation ranging from 10 microns to 0.5 microns, and invstigating sensing capabilities in multimode regime of visible spectrum.

In-situ Characterization of Optical Micro/nano fibers

There are different ways to characterise the delicate optical nanofibers. The characterisation is essential to optimize and explore its uses. In this project, a nondestructive method is employed across various sizes of these fibers. It addresses the issues like simplicity, reusability, risk-free and costeffectiveness which were not addressed as whole in earlier proposed methods.

Introduction to Time Crystals

Time crystals are a fascinating concept in condensed matter physics. They represent a quantum system of particles whose lowest-energy state is one in which the particles are in repetitive motion. In regular crystals, atoms are arranged periodically in space. However, in a time crystal, atoms are arranged periodically in both space and time. Time crystals are a unique phase of matter that exhibit periodic behavior in time, providing a fascinating area of study in the field of quantum physics. During the project various aspects of these crystals were studied.

Fabrication and Analysis of Tapered Optical Micro/nano fibers

Optical Micro/nano fibers has proven to be a vital tool for various quantum technologies. A cost effective fabrication technique using strong acid was adapted. The prepared samples were characterised and compared against their counterparts favricated using more sophisticated techniques like heat and pull. The power of propogating modes inside the fiber was studied. A technique was proposed for experimental studies citing the results of the simulations obtained. Preeliminary experiments were conducted to test the test the ambiguities due to experiments.

Construction of a confocal microscope for single photon studies

Single photon sources has been subject of interest since photonic systems for quantum computation has been in demand. It is essential that we study the photons emitted by these emitters. To facilitate such studies, confocal microscopes are essentials. It can precisely capture the depth from which the photons are being emitted, thereby giving a three dimensional position of such emitters. These studies are essentials for practical realisations of photonic quantum computers.

Introduction to Quantum error correcting codes

Quantum error correction codes are designed to protect quantum information from errors due to decoherence and other quantum noise. These codes work by spreading the information contained in a quantum bit (qubit) across a larger system of qubits. By doing so, they enable the detection and correction of errors without disturbing the information. The project focussed mainly on basics of quantum information and error correction code.
